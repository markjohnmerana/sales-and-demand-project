📊 Sales & Demand Analytics Dashboard

This project analyzes historical sales data to uncover patterns in customer demand, product performance, and revenue trends. The dashboard applies the four types of analytics (Descriptive, Diagnostic, Predictive, and Prescriptive) to help businesses understand past performance, identify the causes of changes in demand, anticipate future sales, and make data-driven decisions.

The goal of this project is to simulate how a data analyst supports business decision-making by transforming raw sales data into actionable insights through SQL modeling, exploratory analysis, and an interactive dashboard.

🎯 Business Problem

Companies often struggle to answer key questions such as:

Which products generate the most revenue?

Why did sales increase or decline during certain periods?

What demand patterns should be expected in the future?

How should the company adjust inventory or promotions?

This project addresses these challenges by building an end-to-end analytics workflow that converts raw sales data into insights that support sales strategy, inventory planning, and demand forecasting.

🔎 Types of Analytics Implemented
1. Descriptive Analytics — What happened?

Provides an overview of historical sales performance.

Key metrics:

Total revenue

Total orders

Top performing products

Sales by region and category

Monthly revenue trends

Business Value:
Helps managers quickly understand overall business performance and identify high-performing products or markets.

2. Diagnostic Analytics — Why did it happen?

Investigates the causes behind trends or anomalies in sales.

Analysis includes:

Sales comparison across regions

Product category performance

Seasonal demand patterns

Revenue contribution by product

Business Value:
Identifies the drivers behind sales growth or decline, helping teams understand which factors influence demand.

3. Predictive Analytics — What will likely happen?

Uses historical patterns to estimate future sales demand.

Approach:

Time-based trend analysis

Demand pattern recognition

Sales forecasting based on historical growth

Business Value:
Supports better inventory planning, demand forecasting, and financial projections.

4. Prescriptive Analytics — What should we do?

Provides recommendations based on the insights discovered.

Examples:

Focus marketing on high-growth product categories

Increase stock for consistently high-demand products

Optimize promotions during peak sales periods

Reduce inventory risk for low-performing products

Business Value:
Transforms insights into actionable strategies that improve revenue and operational efficiency.

🛠 Tech Stack

SQL – Data modeling and querying

Excel / Python (EDA) – Data cleaning and exploration

Power BI – Interactive dashboard visualization

Python - Data validation and machine learning

📈 Dashboard Outcome

The final dashboard allows stakeholders to:

Monitor sales performance in real time

Understand the drivers of demand

Forecast potential future sales trends using machine learning

Make strategic business decisions based on data
